Relevant official points from the current IRS materials:

- A foreign-owned U.S. disregarded entity is treated as separate from its owner and classified as a corporation only for the limited section 6038A reporting requirements that apply to 25% foreign-owned domestic corporations.
- Form 5472 is generally filed when a reporting corporation had a reportable transaction with a foreign or domestic related party.
- A foreign-owned U.S. DE with a Form 5472 requirement must file a pro forma Form 1120 with Form 5472 attached by the Form 1120 due date, including extensions.
- For the foreign-owned U.S. DE pro forma Form 1120, the Form 5472 instructions say only the DE name/address and items B and E on Form 1120 are required.
- The Form 5472 instructions give a dedicated foreign-owned U.S. DE filing route: write `Foreign-owned U.S. DE` across the top of Form 1120 and file by fax or by the dedicated Ogden address.
- The IRS states foreign-owned U.S. DE filers cannot file Form 5472 electronically.

When official IRS instructions differ from the imported Feishu wiki, follow the official current IRS instructions and note the conflict.
